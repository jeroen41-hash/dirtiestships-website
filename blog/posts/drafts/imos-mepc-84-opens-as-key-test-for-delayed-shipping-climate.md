MEPC 84, the 84th session of the International Maritime Organization’s (IMO) Marine Environment Protection Committee, convenes next week under intense pressure to navigate a path forward on shipping's decarbonization, despite the setback of last year’s delayed Net-Zero Framework. While a final decision on the framework is not expected until later this year, the meeting is a critical test of whether consensus can be rebuilt, especially given the significant division revealed by October's vote. The outcome will have ramifications for shipping companies assessed by our [company rankings](/companies.html) and the viability of long term investments.

## High Stakes for Greenhouse Gas Reduction Talks

Agenda Item 7, dedicated to reducing greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions from ships, will dominate the proceedings. With 57 submissions tabled, the debate highlights both the complexity and the critical importance of the issue. A dedicated working group is expected to be established to tackle the range of proposals. The discussions will be informed by recent intersessional GHG talks which covered key areas like lifecycle fuel assessments, an increasingly important aspect of the [CII ratings](/cii.html), and the terms of reference for the upcoming fifth IMO GHG study.

The core challenge remains bridging the deep divisions that led to last year's adjournment. European states are advocating for the framework's carbon-pricing mechanism, while other states, like Japan, are exploring alternative approaches. The United States remains opposed, highlighting the difficulty in achieving a global consensus on mechanisms to tackle emissions.

## Industry Calls for Clarity

Industry bodies, including BIMCO, the International Chamber of Shipping (ICS), and the World Shipping Council (WSC), have jointly emphasized the need for clarity and regulatory certainty. Prolonged uncertainty, they argue, will undermine investment decisions as shipping companies grapple with significant capital commitments required for low- and zero-emission fuels. This sentiment underscores the practical challenges facing companies as they aim to improve their performance and comply with evolving regulations like the EU's Emissions Trading System (ETS) and the IMO's [CII ratings](/cii.html).

## Beyond Climate: Expanded Emissions Controls

MEPC 84's agenda extends beyond GHG reductions. Amendments to MARPOL Annex VI are slated for consideration, including designating the North-East Atlantic as an Emission Control Area (ECA) for nitrogen oxides (NOx), sulphur oxides (SOx), and particulate matter (PM). This ECA designation represents a significant expansion of emissions controls, potentially impacting shipping routes and fuel choices in the region. The agenda also includes changes related to the IMO fuel consumption data collection system and operational carbon intensity reporting. These changes will ultimately be reflected in data available through the EMSA MRV system, crucial for sites like DirtiestShips.com.

## Broader Environmental Agenda

In addition to emissions-focused discussions, MEPC 84 will address a range of other environmental issues, including ballast water management, marine plastic litter, plastic pellet pollution, energy efficiency, air pollution prevention, and underwater radiated noise. This breadth highlights the IMO's commitment to a comprehensive approach to environmental protection in the maritime sector.

## What this means for shipping emissions

MEPC 84 represents a critical juncture for the shipping industry's decarbonization efforts. While divisions remain, the meeting provides an opportunity to rebuild consensus and set a clear path forward. The outcome will directly impact the pace of emissions reductions, influence investment decisions, and shape the regulatory landscape for years to come, affecting the carbon costs associated with the EU ETS. Regardless of the outcome on the Net-Zero Framework, the work on other environmental regulations underscores that the IMO continues to advance environmental protection in the maritime sector.

## FuelEU Maritime: A Crash Course

FuelEU Maritime, a key pillar of the EU's 'Fit for 55' package, sets progressively stricter targets for the greenhouse gas (GHG) intensity of energy used onboard ships calling at EU ports. This regulation compels shipowners to reduce the average GHG intensity of energy used by their vessels by 2% in 2025, escalating to 80% by 2050, relative to a 2020 baseline.

Unlike the [EU ETS](/news.html), which primarily focuses on CO2 emissions, FuelEU Maritime encompasses a broader range of GHGs, including methane and nitrous oxide, further incentivizing the adoption of alternative fuels and energy-efficient technologies. The penalties for non-compliance can be substantial, including fines and, potentially, the exclusion of vessels from EU ports.

## Geogas Tackles Compliance with LR Advisory

According to shipandbunker.com, Geogas Trading has appointed LR Advisory to develop a multi-year emissions compliance plan and operational approach to FuelEU Maritime pooling. This partnership signifies the importance of proactive planning and expert guidance in navigating the intricate regulatory landscape. The announcement underscores the growing demand for advisory services to help shipping companies understand and implement strategies for FuelEU compliance.

The article notes that LR Advisory will guide Geogas on the commercial impact of current and upcoming emissions rules and support in setting up its own FuelEU pooling structure. Pooling, a key mechanism under FuelEU Maritime, allows companies to comply by selling their surplus carbon reductions to others. This can benefit companies that have already invested in low-emission technologies or fuels.

## Understanding FuelEU Pooling

The pooling mechanism under FuelEU Maritime offers flexibility to shipping companies by enabling them to collectively meet their GHG intensity reduction targets. For example, a company that exceeds its reduction target can sell the excess GHG credits to another company struggling to meet its own target. This creates a market-based incentive for early adopters of cleaner technologies and fuels.

LR Advisory’s role in assisting Geogas in setting up its own pooling structure suggests that Geogas intends to actively participate in this market, potentially both as a buyer and seller of GHG credits. Successful implementation of pooling requires careful planning, robust monitoring, and accurate reporting of GHG emissions data, all areas where advisory services can provide valuable support.

## The Commercial Exposure to FuelEU

The shipandbunker.com article states that LR Advisory will assist in mapping and mitigating commercial exposure linked to FuelEU requirements as the European regulatory framework tightens. This is a critical aspect of FuelEU compliance. The costs associated with FuelEU, including investments in alternative fuels, technology upgrades, and the purchase of GHG credits, can significantly impact a company's bottom line. Identifying and mitigating these commercial risks is essential for ensuring the long-term viability of shipping operations. Companies may need to adjust their business models, fleet deployment strategies, and pricing structures to account for the costs of FuelEU compliance. Furthermore, access to [emissions data](/charts.html) is crucial for informed decision-making and accurate risk assessment.

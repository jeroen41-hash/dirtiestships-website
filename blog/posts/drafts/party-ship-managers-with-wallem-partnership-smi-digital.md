The implementation of EU Emissions Trading System (ETS) and upcoming FuelEU Maritime regulations is forcing ship managers to adopt sophisticated, system-based approaches to emissions compliance. Wallem Group's recent partnership with OceanScore to manage EU ETS and FuelEU Maritime obligations is the latest indicator that emissions compliance is rapidly shifting from a reporting exercise to a core commercial function. This transition demands reliable data management, transparent cost allocation, and efficient execution across diverse fleets – capabilities that third-party managers increasingly require.

## Wallem Selects OceanScore for Emissions Compliance

According to a recent article from shipmanagementinternational.com, Wallem Group has chosen OceanScore's Compliance Manager to oversee EU ETS and FuelEU Maritime compliance for its fleet calling at EU ports. This includes access to OceanScore's FuelEU Pooling Marketplace. This partnership follows similar agreements between OceanScore and other major ship management companies, including Anglo-Eastern, V.Group, V-Ships, and Wilhelmsen Ahrenkiel, signalling a growing trend towards structured emissions management within the industry. The article highlights the increasing commercial responsibilities placed on third-party managers due to the EU ETS and FuelEU Maritime regulations, requiring them to manage exposure, cost allocation, and invoicing on behalf of owners and charterers.

## The Rise of System-Based Compliance

OceanScore's solution aims to address the complexity of managing emissions compliance across large, diverse fleets. The company claims its platform provides structured, data-based workflows that enable scale, transparency, and consistent execution. Albrecht Grell, Managing Director of OceanScore, emphasizes the central role of third-party managers in compliance execution, stating that they require systems that work reliably across vessels, owners, and charterers. The shift towards system-based compliance underscores the limitations of manual processes in the face of increasingly stringent regulations. It also reflects the need for accurate data to inform decision-making and minimize financial risks associated with emissions trading. This type of emissions management software is also increasingly important in assessing how a ship will fare with its [CII ratings](/cii.html), given the need to model and predict future fuel consumption based on a variety of operational parameters.

## OceanScore's Expanding Market Presence

The partnership with Wallem further solidifies OceanScore's position as a leading provider of emissions compliance solutions. OceanScore reports supporting over 100 customers and 2,500 vessels globally. The company's annual recurring revenue has reached USD 5 million, indicating the growing demand for commercially focused compliance infrastructure. In addition to its work with ship managers, OceanScore has also been selected by the International Association of Ports and Harbours (IAPH) to administer the Environmental Ship Index (ESI), further expanding its role within the maritime industry.

## The Financial Implications of Compliance

The article points to the financial implications of emissions compliance, noting the need for reliable cost allocation, invoicing, and settlement processes. As the EU ETS extends to shipping, companies face significant carbon costs, which can impact their profitability and competitiveness. The [EU ETS carbon costs](/charts.html) will vary significantly based on vessel type, route, and fuel consumption, making accurate data and efficient management crucial. Ship management companies like Wallem are seeking solutions like OceanScore to navigate the complexities of the EU ETS and FuelEU Maritime regulations and minimize financial risks for their clients. This also has implications for [company rankings](/companies.html) based on emissions performance, as those with efficient emissions management strategies will likely gain a competitive advantage.

## What this means for shipping emissions

The adoption of system-based emissions compliance solutions by major ship management companies marks a significant step towards reducing greenhouse gas emissions from the shipping industry. By providing accurate data, transparent cost allocation, and efficient execution, these solutions can help companies minimize their carbon footprint and comply with increasingly stringent regulations. As the EU ETS and FuelEU Maritime enter their execution phases, the demand for such solutions is likely to continue to grow, further driving the transition towards a more sustainable shipping industry.

The shipping industry is under increasing pressure to decarbonize, with regulations like the EU ETS and IMO targets looming large. A new partnership between classification society ABS and container line PIL aims to improve the accuracy and verification of emissions reductions claims associated with alternative fuel use, potentially influencing the uptake of these fuels and impacting [CII ratings](/cii.html) across PIL's fleet.

## ABS and PIL Partner on Emissions Verification

According to hellenicshippingnews.com, ABS and Pacific International Lines (PIL) have signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to collaborate on verifying emissions reductions claims derived from the use of alternative marine fuels. The partnership will focus on PIL’s participation in “Book and Claim” registries, ensuring independent verification of emissions reductions.

ABS will act as a third-party verifier, assessing PIL’s fuel consumption, transport activity, and emissions data. The goal is to provide a rigorous and transparent oversight mechanism for “Book and Claim” schemes, which allow companies to claim emissions reductions even if the physical low-carbon fuel isn't directly used in their own operations. This is crucial for building confidence in these schemes and encouraging wider adoption of alternative fuels.

## The Importance of Accurate Emissions Data

The credibility of emissions reduction claims is paramount, especially as the industry navigates increasingly stringent regulations like the EU ETS. The EU MRV (Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification) regulation, which forms the basis for our data on [emissions data](/charts.html), already mandates detailed emissions reporting for ships calling at EU ports. This ABS/PIL collaboration strengthens the data integrity component, particularly in the context of alternative fuels.

Without accurate and verified data, it’s impossible to accurately assess the true impact of decarbonization efforts or determine a vessel's [CII rating](/cii.html). A poor CII rating can impact a vessel’s commercial viability, potentially leading to lower charter rates or even premature scrapping. Third-party verification, such as that provided by ABS, is crucial for ensuring that these ratings are based on reliable information.

## Book and Claim: A Bridge to Alternative Fuels?

"Book and Claim" systems are designed to accelerate the adoption of sustainable fuels by decoupling the physical flow of the fuel from the emissions reduction claim. For example, a company could purchase sustainable fuel at a port where it's readily available and claim the emissions reduction benefit, even if the physical fuel is used by another vessel elsewhere. This can incentivize the production and availability of alternative fuels, even if infrastructure and distribution networks are still developing.

However, the success of "Book and Claim" hinges on robust verification processes. Without independent oversight, the system is vulnerable to greenwashing, where companies make unsubstantiated claims about their emissions reductions. ABS's role as a third-party verifier helps address this concern, adding credibility to PIL's participation in such schemes.

## Implications for PIL's Fleet and [Company Rankings](/companies.html)

This initiative could have a significant impact on PIL's fleet profile and potentially improve its position in [company rankings](/companies.html) on DirtiestShips.com. By accurately tracking and verifying emissions reductions from alternative fuels, PIL can demonstrate its commitment to decarbonization and potentially improve the CII ratings of its vessels. This, in turn, could enhance the company's reputation and attract environmentally conscious customers. It will be interesting to track how this initiative impacts PIL's reported emissions data in the EMSA MRV database and consequently, its ranking relative to other container lines. The article does not specify which fuel types will be verified under the MOU.

## What this means for shipping emissions

The collaboration between ABS and PIL underscores the growing importance of accurate and transparent emissions data in the shipping industry. As regulations tighten and stakeholders demand greater accountability, initiatives like this that focus on verifiable emissions reductions will become increasingly critical. While "Book and Claim" systems offer a potential pathway to accelerate the adoption of alternative fuels, their success depends on robust verification mechanisms. This partnership represents a step in the right direction, promoting greater confidence and transparency in emissions reduction claims.

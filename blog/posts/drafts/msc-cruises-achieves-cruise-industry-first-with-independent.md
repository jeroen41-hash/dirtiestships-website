MSC Cruises has achieved a significant milestone, becoming the first cruise line to secure independent verification and flag state recognition of actual methane emissions from its ships under the FuelEU Maritime regulations. This development marks a shift towards more accurate and transparent emissions reporting in the cruise sector and the wider shipping industry, moving away from default assumptions to real-world performance data.

## Measured Methane Slip Significantly Below FuelEU Default

According to a press release from MSC Cruises, the verification process, conducted in collaboration with Bureau Veritas Marine & Offshore, involved measuring methane slip from the dual-fuel LNG engines of *MSC World Europa* and *MSC Euribia*. The results showed a methane slip value of 1.67% for *MSC World Europa* and 1.48% for *MSC Euribia*. These figures are notably lower than the 3.1% default value specified in FuelEU Maritime Annex II. This difference is crucial because FuelEU Maritime mandates gradual reductions in the greenhouse gas (GHG) intensity of energy used onboard ships calling at EU ports, and accurate methane measurement is essential for demonstrating compliance.

The company press release notes that the methodology employed for the verification included engine-specific methane emissions files, a dedicated methane record book, defined engine load monitoring procedures, and onboard exhaust gas measurements using multiple measurement devices, aligned with IMO Resolution MEPC.402(83) and FuelEU Maritime Guidelines. The press release emphasized that the findings support better regulatory decision-making as the maritime sector aims for net-zero GHG emissions.

## Implications for FuelEU Maritime and EU ETS

The ability to report independently verified methane emissions has significant implications for MSC Cruises’ compliance with both FuelEU Maritime and the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS). FuelEU Maritime focuses on the lifecycle GHG intensity of fuels used onboard ships, while the EU ETS now includes maritime emissions, requiring shipowners to surrender allowances for each tonne of CO2 equivalent emitted.

By using actual, verified methane emissions data, MSC Cruises can potentially reduce its reported GHG intensity under FuelEU Maritime, leading to lower compliance costs. Similarly, under the EU ETS, more accurate methane measurement can impact the total CO2 equivalent emissions for which the company must purchase allowances. The company has not released specific data for how the lower methane slip values impact either its FuelEU compliance trajectory or EU ETS costs, but the implications are clear. This approach will likely encourage other cruise lines and shipowners to adopt similar verification processes. The company also released a Statement of Compliance, formally recognizing the verified results and supporting alignment with EU ETS and FuelEU Maritime requirements.

## Broader Impact on Shipping Industry

MSC Cruises' initiative could serve as a catalyst for greater transparency and accuracy in methane emissions reporting across the shipping industry. While the [CII ratings](/cii.html) largely focus on operational efficiency and CO2 emissions, the increasing scrutiny on methane slip from LNG-fueled vessels makes accurate measurement imperative.

Methane, a potent greenhouse gas, has a significantly higher global warming potential than CO2 over a shorter timeframe. The EU's regulatory framework is increasingly focused on full lifecycle emissions, and verified data on methane slip is becoming essential for assessing the true environmental impact of LNG as a transitional fuel. This move towards verified emissions highlights a growing need for robust measurement technologies and standardized reporting protocols across the industry. Without these, companies face the risk of penalties or reputational damage if default values paint a misleading picture of their environmental performance. It's worth watching to see how this development affects [company rankings](/companies.html) and other benchmarks that factor in overall GHG emissions.

## What this means for shipping emissions

MSC Cruises' achievement in independently verifying methane emissions represents a crucial step towards more accurate and transparent environmental reporting within the shipping industry. By replacing default assumptions with real-world data, the company strengthens the credibility of emissions reporting under FuelEU Maritime and EU ETS. This move not only supports informed regulatory decision-making but also incentivizes the adoption of advanced technologies and operational improvements to minimize methane slip from LNG-fueled vessels. As the maritime sector progresses toward net-zero emissions, such initiatives are essential for ensuring the effectiveness of decarbonization strategies and promoting a more sustainable future for shipping.

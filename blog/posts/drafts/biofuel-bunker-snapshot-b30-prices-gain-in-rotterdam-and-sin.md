The price fluctuations of biofuel bunker blends in key ports like Rotterdam and Singapore offer a glimpse into the complex interplay of regulatory incentives, feedstock costs, and fluctuating demand impacting shipping's decarbonization efforts. While biofuels represent a potential pathway to reducing greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, their economic viability and widespread adoption hinge on factors such as government subsidies, feedstock availability, and the evolving carbon pricing landscape.

## B30 Price Movements: Rotterdam vs. Singapore

According to a recent report from hellenicshippingnews.com, Rotterdam experienced a $28/mt increase in the price of B30-VLSFO (POMEME) this week. In contrast, the price of conventional VLSFO remained steady. This rise occurred despite a drop in the price of ZRE A tickets, which are generated by suppliers delivering low-carbon biofuel blends in Dutch ports and can be sold to conventional fuel suppliers as an incentive. The price drop in these tickets, from €125/mtCO2e to €115/mtCO2e, suggests a possible oversupply in the market, potentially indicating that demand for biofuel is outpacing the compliance demand for the associated certificates. This dynamic could be contributing to the increased B30 price in Rotterdam.

Meanwhile, Singapore saw a significant rise in its B30-VLSFO (UCOME) price, fueled by a $49/mt increase in the price of conventional VLSFO. Interestingly, the price of China UCOME cargo, a key feedstock for Singapore's B30 blend, decreased by $15/mt over the same period. Despite this, spot bunker demand for biofuel blends in Singapore remains low, according to the article. This low demand, coupled with fluctuating feedstock prices, creates uncertainty in the region's biofuel market.

## The Role of Regulatory Incentives: ZRE Tickets in Rotterdam

Rotterdam's biofuel market is significantly influenced by the ZRE A ticket system. These tickets, awarded to suppliers of low-carbon advanced biofuel blends, provide a financial incentive that can subsidize the price of biofuels compared to other ports. However, the report notes that the ZRE market remains small with limited participants, affecting price discovery and potentially creating volatility. The fluctuating prices of these tickets, as tracked in our [emissions data](/charts.html), highlight the importance of stable and predictable regulatory frameworks in supporting the adoption of alternative fuels. These incentives can help to improve the [CII ratings](/cii.html) for vessels using biofuels.

## Book-and-Claim and HVO Testing

The article also touches on two other interesting developments. First, Kuehne+Nagel will claim nearly 3,000 mt of CO2-equivalent GHG emissions reductions from Hapag-Lloyd’s use of biofuel under a book-and-claim agreement. While this approach can incentivize biofuel use, its effectiveness depends on robust verification and transparency to ensure that emissions reductions are accurately accounted for. Second, MSC Cruises has tested pure hydrogenated vegetable oil (HVO100) on one of its cruise ships. This fuel, produced from waste-based feedstocks, demonstrates the potential of using sustainable sources to reduce shipping's carbon footprint.

## Biofuels and the EU ETS

The increasing adoption of biofuels will also be impacted by the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS). As shipping is now included in the EU ETS, companies will face carbon costs for their emissions. Biofuels that meet the sustainability criteria of the Renewable Energy Directive (RED) can contribute to reducing a company's ETS liability. Therefore, biofuels offer a way to reduce the financial burden imposed by the EU ETS, potentially influencing fuel choices. This is particularly relevant for companies whose [company rankings](/companies.html) show high emissions profiles. The EMSA MRV data which informs our platform is key to tracking the uptake of such alternative fuels.

## What this means for shipping emissions

The biofuel market remains dynamic and sensitive to a range of factors, including regulatory incentives, feedstock costs, and demand fluctuations. While biofuels offer a pathway to reduce shipping emissions, their widespread adoption hinges on creating stable and transparent markets, supported by robust regulatory frameworks. Monitoring these price trends and policy developments is crucial for understanding the potential role of biofuels in achieving the industry's decarbonization goals and improving overall environmental performance.

## Fleet Renewal and Energy-Saving Technologies

Performance Shipping currently operates a fleet of twelve tanker vessels, with ten actively sailing and two newbuilds scheduled for delivery in early 2026 and early 2027. The company says its fleet renewal program demonstrates a commitment to sustainability (businessfocusmagazine.com). The focus on modernizing the fleet ties directly into improving [CII ratings](/cii.html) and reducing exposure to EU ETS carbon costs. Newer vessels typically achieve higher energy efficiency, leading to lower fuel consumption and reduced CO2 emissions.

Furthermore, Performance Shipping states it is installing energy-saving devices to improve hydrodynamic characteristics, enhance fuel efficiency, and measurably reduce emissions (businessfocusmagazine.com). Specific types of energy-saving devices aren't mentioned, but could include things like propeller boss cap fins or air lubrication systems. The article references the use of voyage optimization software and speed management practices, both of which are relatively low-cost ways to enhance vessel efficiency.

## Regulatory Compliance and Transparency

CEO Andreas Michalopoulos emphasizes Performance Shipping's proactive approach to navigating "increasingly complex environmental and regulatory requirements" (businessfocusmagazine.com). The company is investing in emissions-monitoring technologies to ensure transparent reporting in line with EU ETS and FuelEU Maritime regulations. This transparency is not only essential for compliance but also for building trust with charterers and investors who are increasingly scrutinizing the environmental performance of shipping companies.

The EU ETS, in particular, places a direct cost on CO2 emissions from ships calling at EU ports. FuelEU Maritime, commencing in 2025, sets progressively tightening limits on the greenhouse gas intensity of energy used onboard ships.

The calculus for shipping companies navigating the EU's complex web of environmental regulations is shifting dramatically. Recent data indicates a surge in FuelEU Maritime pooling values for biofuels like B100 and liquefied biomethane (LBM). This means that shipowners utilizing these fuels could see a significant increase in the financial rewards – and reduced costs – for doing so. The implications for fleet decarbonization strategies and compliance with regulations like FuelEU Maritime and the EU ETS are substantial.

## Biofuel Pooling Values Skyrocket

According to a recent report, FuelEU Maritime pooling values have experienced a significant jump, with OceanScore’s FuelEU pooling index climbing €36/mtCO2e in a single week (hellenicshippingnews.com). This surge directly translates to higher potential revenue for shipowners who opt for lower-emission fuels.

Specifically, the potential pooling value of B100 (biodiesel) for EU-EU voyages has risen by $113/mt, reaching $710/mt. Liquefied biomethane (LBM) has seen an even more dramatic increase, with potential pooling values for EU-EU voyages jumping by $157-185/mt to between $985-1,155/mt, depending on the engine type. For voyages between the EU and non-EU ports, the increase in B100's pooling value is smaller, at $57/mt, bringing the total to $355/mt. These numbers suggest a stronger incentive for ships operating primarily within the EU to adopt B100 or LBM.

This increase in pooling values is linked to FuelEU Maritime, which incentivizes the use of lower-carbon fuels through a crediting system. Vessels exceeding the required greenhouse gas intensity reduction can generate "compliance surplus" that can be sold to vessels that are struggling to meet their targets. The higher the pooling value, the greater the financial benefit for ships using compliant fuels.

## Price Dynamics and Fuel Cost Impact

The surge in pooling values is impacting the price dynamics between conventional and bio-based fuels. In Rotterdam, the discount of B100 to Very Low Sulphur Fuel Oil (VLSFO) has deepened by $163/mt, now reaching $375/mt. The B100 discount to Low Sulphur Marine Gas Oil (LSMGO) has widened by $99/mt, to $822/mt. LBM discounts to LSMGO have also expanded significantly, by $96-122/mt, to $979-1,208/mt, while discounts to LNG have risen $153/mt higher to $524-530/mt, depending on engine type (hellenicshippingnews.com).

The interplay between pooling values and fuel prices is complex. For example, while Rotterdam's B100 price dropped $107/mt for EU-EU voyages, the rise in FuelEU potential pooling values effectively reduces the net cost of B100 bunkered in Rotterdam and used on EU-EU voyages to $722/mt (hellenicshippingnews.com). This demonstrates how regulatory incentives can significantly alter the economic viability of alternative fuels.

## Regional Variations: Rotterdam vs. Singapore

The market for biofuels is not uniform across the globe. While Rotterdam sees deepening discounts for B100 compared to conventional fuels, Singapore presents a different picture. In Singapore, B100's premium over VLSFO has narrowed sharply, by $105/mt, to $154/mt (hellenicshippingnews.com). This difference reflects variations in fuel availability, local regulations, and demand for lower-emission fuels in different regions. ## LNG Market Volatility

The report also highlights volatility in the LNG market, driven by geopolitical factors. The ongoing closure of the Strait of Hormuz, which disrupts LNG supply, has led to rallies in gas prices (hellenicshippingnews.com). This highlights the vulnerability of LNG as a bunker fuel to external events.

However, the article points out that LNG bunkered by vessels with diesel slow speed (diesel SS) engines can generate a significant compliance surplus due to the engine’s low methane slip. This is an important factor, as methane slip – the release of uncombusted methane – is a major concern with LNG as a marine fuel.

## What this means for shipping emissions

The increasing value of FuelEU Maritime pooling is a clear signal that the transition to lower-emission fuels is gaining momentum. This incentivizes shipowners to consider B100 and LBM, impacting their [CII ratings](/cii.html) and costs under the EU ETS. While challenges remain – including fuel availability and price volatility – the financial incentives are now more compelling. The rise in pooling values puts further pressure on companies with lower [company rankings](/companies.html) in carbon emissions. As more data becomes available through EMSA MRV and is analyzed on platforms like [emissions data](/charts.html), the industry will gain a clearer picture of the true cost and benefits of adopting alternative fuels.

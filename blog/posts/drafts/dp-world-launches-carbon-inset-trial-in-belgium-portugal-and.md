DP World's launch of "Insetify" – a carbon insetting trial for ocean freight customers in Belgium, Portugal, and Sweden – highlights the growing pressure on shipping companies to address Scope 3 emissions and demonstrates one approach to managing the carbon footprint of sea transport within the existing value chain. While the trial's scope is currently limited, it signals a potential shift in how companies are addressing emissions beyond simply purchasing carbon offsets.

## Insetting vs. Offsetting: A Key Distinction

The DP World initiative emphasizes "carbon insetting" rather than traditional "carbon offsetting." According to the announcement, insetting involves reducing emissions *within* a company's or its value chain, for example, through the use of lower-carbon fuels or more efficient transport. This contrasts with offsetting, which typically funds projects outside the value chain, such as reforestation. The Carbon Inset Credits in the Insetify trial are generated by DP World Shipping Solutions through the deployment of incrementally lower carbon fuel. This focus on direct emissions reduction within the shipping process is increasingly viewed as a more robust and verifiable approach to decarbonization compared to traditional offsets.

## Details of the Insetify Trial

Starting April 1st, the Insetify trial automatically applies credits to customers booking ocean freight forwarding services with DP World in the specified countries. Qualifying customers (reaching a threshold of 25 TEUs per quarter) will receive Carbon Inset Credits at a rate of 100 kg CO2e per TEU shipped per quarter, at no additional cost. To illustrate, a customer shipping 50 TEUs quarterly will receive credits equal to 5,000 kg (5t CO2e). This program builds upon a similar initiative launched in January 2025 at DP World's UK ports (Southampton and London Gateway), which reportedly registered over 250,000 TEUs and issued more than 9,000 tCO2e of Carbon Inset Credits. However, the article doesn't mention the cost of the fuel.

## Scope 3 Emissions and the Regulatory Landscape

The Insetify trial is specifically targeted at reducing customers' Scope 3 emissions. Scope 3 emissions are indirect emissions resulting from a company's activities, occurring from sources that they do not own or control. For shipping companies, this includes emissions from the transportation of goods they handle. Addressing Scope 3 emissions is becoming increasingly important as companies face greater scrutiny from investors, customers, and regulators. This focus could become more important for [CII ratings](/cii.html) as companies are more holistically evaluated, especially given how they encourage customers to choose cleaner options.

While the article doesn't explicitly connect Insetify to the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS), the broader context of European shipping regulations is crucial. Since January 2024, the EU ETS has expanded to include maritime transport, requiring shipowners to purchase allowances for a portion of their emissions on voyages to, from, and within the EU. Initiatives like Insetify, though currently modest in scale, could potentially influence a company's overall emissions profile and therefore its [EU ETS carbon costs](/charts.html).

## Implications for Company Emissions Rankings

This initiative, even if primarily symbolic at this stage, could potentially influence DP World's standing in future [company rankings](/companies.html) assessing shipping companies' environmental performance. However, the scale of impact is currently unclear. While 5 tonnes of CO2e offset per 50 TEUs shipped quarterly sounds good, without more data the actual carbon intensity reductions are not clear. This program is offered to customers at no cost and covers a limited geographic area.

## What this means for shipping emissions

DP World's Insetify trial, while limited in scope, represents a noteworthy step towards integrating emissions reduction efforts within the shipping value chain. It underscores the increasing emphasis on Scope 3 emissions and the potential for carbon insetting to become a more prevalent strategy in the industry. This approach, alongside advancements in alternative fuels and vessel efficiency, will be critical for the shipping sector to meet its ambitious decarbonization targets. While Insetify is still a trial and relatively small, it can be a starting point for shipping companies that are trying to get ahead of regulatory demands.

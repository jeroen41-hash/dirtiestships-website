FuelEU Maritime: Geogas Trading Prepares for Pooling with LR Advisory

The shipping industry is bracing for FuelEU Maritime, a regulation poised to significantly impact vessel operations and fuel choices. As the first compliance cycle approaches, companies are actively seeking strategies to navigate the complexities of the regulation, particularly the mechanism of emissions pooling. Geogas Trading's recent partnership with LR Advisory underscores this growing need for expert guidance.

FuelEU Maritime and the Lure of Emissions Pooling

According to hellenicshippingnews.com, Geogas Trading, a Geneva-based LPG trader and shipowner, has enlisted Lloyd's Register (LR) Advisory to develop a multi-year emissions compliance plan focusing on FuelEU Maritime's pooling mechanism. FuelEU Maritime, part of the EU's "Fit for 55" package, aims to reduce the greenhouse gas intensity of energy used onboard ships calling at EU ports. Vessels must gradually decrease the GHG intensity of their energy use, starting in 2025.

One key aspect of FuelEU Maritime is the possibility of pooling compliance. This allows multiple ships to combine their GHG performance, potentially offsetting vessels exceeding the limits with those performing better. This pooling mechanism can offer flexibility and cost optimization, but requires careful planning and execution. The source article indicates that LR Advisory will assist Geogas in designing and implementing its own FuelEU Maritime pooling structure, developing the operational frameworks needed to execute it effectively.

Commercial Implications and Risk Mitigation

The article highlights that LR Advisory will provide "expert insight into the commercial implications of current and forthcoming emissions regulations" and assist in mapping and mitigating commercial risks associated with FuelEU. This is crucial, as FuelEU introduces a direct link between emissions performance and financial outcomes. As Luke Shu of LR Advisory notes, charterers and shipowners face "incentives and penalties that influence day-to-day operational, chartering decisions and P&L."

Understanding the commercial implications of regulations like FuelEU is vital for informed decision-making and strategic planning.

FuelEU, CII and EU ETS: A Complex Regulatory Landscape

While the article focuses on FuelEU Maritime, it's important to understand it within the broader context of EU regulations affecting shipping emissions. FuelEU operates alongside the existing EU Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (MRV) regulation, the [CII ratings](/cii.html) scheme, and the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS). The EU ETS, in particular, now applies to emissions from ships calling at EU ports, adding another layer of carbon cost to vessel operations.

The interplay between these regulations can create complexities. For example, a vessel with a poor [CII rating](/cii.html) might also face higher carbon costs under the EU ETS, further incentivizing emission reduction measures. The addition of FuelEU adds a further driver for ship operators to proactively improve their fleet emission performance.

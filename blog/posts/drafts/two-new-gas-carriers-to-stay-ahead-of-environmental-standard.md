The global shipping industry, under immense pressure to decarbonize, is seeing crucial milestones emerge that redefine its future emissions profile. The recent announcement that two new 51,350 m3 midsize LPG/liquid ammonia carriers will be equipped with Wärtsilä 25 Ammonia auxiliary engines marks a significant leap forward, signaling a tangible commitment to low-emission operations that will profoundly impact future carbon intensity ratings, EU ETS costs, and company environmental standing. This move by Navigator Amon Shipping AS, a joint venture between Navigator Gas and Amon Maritime, underscores the accelerating shift from theoretical net-zero ambitions to concrete, ordered solutions.

## Ammonia-Powered Pioneers: A Glimpse into the Future Fleet

The new vessels, being constructed at Nantong CIMC Sinopacific Offshore & Engineering (SOE) shipyard in Shanghai, are designed from the keel up for a decarbonized future. At their core will be the Wärtsilä 25 Ammonia auxiliary engine, complemented by a NOx reducer (SCR) and gas valve unit (GVU). Wärtsilä, a technology group at the forefront of alternative fuel solutions, will also supply the full propulsion system, including a Controllable Pitch Propulsion solution engineered for optimal efficiency and reliability. The orders for these critical components were booked in Q2 and Q3 2026, indicating a forward-looking procurement strategy that anticipates future regulatory demands. This commitment to advanced propulsion will be a key differentiator when comparing [emissions data](/charts.html) across fleets in the coming years.

## Dramatic Emissions Reductions on the Horizon

The primary driver behind this technological adoption is the profound impact on greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. Wärtsilä states that when running on sustainable ammonia, the Wärtsilä 25 Ammonia engine can reduce total greenhouse gas emissions by "at least 90 percent" compared to equivalent diesel engines. This dramatic reduction aligns directly with the IMO’s ambitious net-zero-emissions target for 2050, positioning ammonia as a leading carbon-free marine fuel pathway. Wärtsilä's proactive development in this space, having introduced the marine sector’s first commercially available 4-stroke engine-based solution for ammonia fuel back in 2023, highlights the maturity of this technology. Such significant cuts in emissions will be immediately evident in the vessel's operational data, providing a tangible benchmark for the industry.

## Navigating the Regulatory Landscape: CII and EU ETS

For platforms like DirtiestShips.com, which scrutinize operational efficiency through metrics like the Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII), these new ammonia-fuelled vessels represent a paradigm shift. Designed for "lower-emission operations," these carriers are inherently positioned to achieve top [CII ratings](/cii.html), likely securing an 'A' grade from day one of operation. This proactive embrace of decarbonization stands in stark contrast to the majority of the global fleet, which is grappling with retrofits and operational adjustments to avoid punitive 'D' or 'E' ratings.

Furthermore, the implications for EU Emissions Trading System (ETS) costs are monumental. With a 90% reduction in GHG emissions, the carbon liability for these vessels operating within EU waters will be drastically cut, if not entirely eliminated, depending on the certified emissions factor for sustainable ammonia. As carbon prices under the EU ETS continue to fluctuate and are projected to rise, avoiding significant carbon costs provides a substantial competitive advantage and strengthens the economic case for early adopters of such technology. This tangible cost saving directly addresses the "evolving regulatory requirements" cited by Navigator Gas's Director of Vessel Operations, Mr. Kaj S. Pilemand. Emissions data collected via the EU MRV system will eventually provide concrete proof of these cost savings and environmental benefits.

## Strategic Investment: Navigator Gas and Amon Maritime's Proactive Stance

The decision by Navigator Amon Shipping AS to invest in these advanced vessels showcases a strategic commitment to environmental leadership. As Mr. Pilemand states, by selecting the Wärtsilä 25 Ammonia engine, they "expect to dramatically reduce the ships’ environmental impact, while supporting compliance with evolving regulatory requirements." This forward-thinking approach will likely see Navigator Gas and Amon Maritime rise in future [company rankings](/companies.html) that evaluate emissions performance and decarbonization efforts. Such investments signal a clear intent to be at the forefront of the industry's green transition, providing a blueprint for other operators navigating the complex path to net-zero. This project, as Wärtsilä's Stefan Nysjö notes, "reflects the continued momentum behind carbon-neutral and zero-carbon fuels, with ammonia emerging as one key pathway."

## What this means for shipping emissions

The order for two ammonia-ready carriers is more than just a new shipbuilding contract; it represents a critical turning point in the decarbonization of global shipping. It solidifies ammonia as a viable and increasingly adopted marine fuel, demonstrating that significant GHG emission reductions are not just aspirational but achievable with commercially available technology. This move will set new benchmarks for CII ratings, drastically reduce EU ETS liabilities, and differentiate companies committed to genuine environmental stewardship. As these vessels enter service, they will offer invaluable real-world data, accelerating the industry's shift towards a cleaner, more sustainable future, and setting a high bar for all future newbuilds.

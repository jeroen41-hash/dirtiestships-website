## DNV Endorsement: A Catalyst for Wind-Assisted Propulsion

The DNV Type Approval, formally presented at Posidonia, confirms bound4blue's largest and most powerful installed suction sail system's compliance with DNV’s rigorous standards for safety, structural integrity, and design robustness. This certification, as highlighted in the Cyprus Shipping News article, is not just a technical formality; it's a critical enabler for wider industry adoption. By reducing uncertainty, it makes investment in WAPS more attractive to regulators, insurers, and charterers. For shipowners, a DNV-certified solution mitigates risk and accelerates the integration of wind power into both newbuild and retrofit decarbonization strategies, making it easier to meet evolving environmental performance targets.

## The Model 3-24 eSAIL: Technical Specifications and Impact

The Model 3-24 eSAIL® represents bound4blue's next-generation technology, designed specifically for larger vessels seeking to maximize wind propulsive contribution. Standing at 24 metres (with the Model 3 range extending to 36 metres), this system features an expanded sail surface area and optimized aerodynamic profile, enhancing efficiency and propulsion output. Its core technology uses boundary layer suction to generate propulsive force, thereby reducing main engine fuel consumption and, consequently, CO2 emissions.

The first installation of two Model 3-24 units on the Klaveness Combination Carriers newbuild MV Baltazar in April demonstrates its real-world application. Such installations directly translate into measurable fuel savings and emissions reductions. From a data perspective, these reductions will be reflected in the vessel's reported emissions via the EU MRV framework, providing concrete evidence of the technology's impact on its environmental footprint.

## Navigating Regulations: CII, ETS, and Future Fuels

The adoption of advanced WAPS like the eSAIL® offers multiple benefits for regulatory compliance. The news article explicitly states that the technology directly contributes to improved [CII ratings](/cii.html) and compliance with EEXI requirements. For vessels struggling to maintain a favorable A, B, or C rating, the consistent propulsive assistance from wind can be a significant factor in avoiding D or E ratings, which trigger mandatory corrective action plans.

Furthermore, reduced fuel consumption directly impacts a vessel's exposure to carbon pricing mechanisms, notably the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS). By lowering CO2 emissions, ships operating in the EU/EEA will generate fewer allowances, translating into tangible cost reductions. This positions wind propulsion as a dual solution: both an emissions reducer and a cost-saving tool in a tightening regulatory environment. The technology also supports the transition to future fuels like methanol and ammonia by reducing the overall energy demand, thereby potentially lessening the required volume of more expensive or challenging alternative fuels.

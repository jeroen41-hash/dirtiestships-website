FuelEU Maritime, set to launch on January 1, 2025, represents a significant challenge for the shipping industry, particularly for ship managers who will be directly liable for financial penalties arising from non-compliance with the regulation's stringent carbon intensity reduction targets. This has spurred innovation in risk management, with new insurance solutions emerging to mitigate the financial burden.

## FuelEU Maritime Places Liability on Ship Managers

The core of FuelEU Maritime lies in its escalating carbon intensity reduction targets, which ratchet down every five years until 2050. These targets aim to drastically cut the maritime sector's greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. However, a key feature that differentiates FuelEU from other environmental regulations is its assignment of compliance responsibility to the Document of Compliance (DoC) holder – typically the ship manager.

According to a recent analysis by Willis Towers Watson (WTW), this presents a unique problem. Many ships are managed by third-party companies acting as agents for the ship owner. Under FuelEU Maritime, these managers become directly liable for financial penalties if the vessel fails to meet the required carbon intensity targets. Standard industry clauses developed by organizations like BIMCO require shipowners to provide collateral, such as bank guarantees, to cover potential penalties. WTW highlights the opportunity for alternative credit risk insurance solutions to reduce the complexity of collateral requirements.

This shift in liability raises concerns about the financial risks faced by ship managers and has led to the development of innovative insurance products designed to protect them against potential losses. This added financial pressure will undoubtedly influence companies' [CII ratings](/cii.html) and their subsequent position in our [company rankings](/companies.html).

## Insurance as a Risk Mitigation Tool

In response to the FuelEU challenge, WTW collaborated with a large ship management organization and Markel, an insurance provider, to develop an insurance solution aimed at reducing the financial risks associated with FuelEU penalties. The insurance solution underwrites the ship manager's potential liabilities and provides a lower-cost alternative to bank guarantees. The insurance scales with the FuelEU exposure, which varies depending on the number of voyages and emissions within the European Union.

The Markel policy provides insurance cover up to 95% of potential exposure to FuelEU penalties in the event that ship owners are unable to pay. This offers a safety net for ship managers facing financial repercussions due to non-compliant vessels.

## Enhanced Credit Management and Contractual Improvements

Beyond simply providing financial cover, the implementation of this insurance solution has spurred improvements in risk management practices. WTW indicates that they assisted their client in enhancing their credit management procedures, reducing the risk of claims and ensuring insurance cover availability. This included developing a framework for managing both current and future client relationships.

Furthermore, the initiative led to contractual improvements between ship managers and ship owners. WTW's client successfully amended its ship management agreements to account for the FuelEU legislation, solidifying the framework for responsibility and compliance.

## Leveraging Captive Insurance Arrangements

The insurance solution also involved leveraging captive insurance arrangements, with Markel acting as the reinsurer. This provided additional protection and cash flow benefits for the ship management company, demonstrating a comprehensive approach to risk management within the context of FuelEU Maritime. The source article is light on specifics here, but this approach suggests a sophisticated understanding of risk transfer and mitigation within the maritime industry.

## What this means for shipping emissions

FuelEU Maritime's imposition of direct financial liability on ship managers for vessel emissions within the EU is a game-changer. While the emergence of insurance solutions offers a buffer against immediate financial risk, the underlying pressure to reduce carbon intensity remains. Ship managers now have a powerful incentive to actively pursue emission reduction strategies, pushing ship owners to invest in cleaner technologies and fuels. Ultimately, this regulatory pressure, coupled with innovative risk management tools, should accelerate the decarbonization of the shipping sector and impact the data we track on our [emissions data](/charts.html) pages.

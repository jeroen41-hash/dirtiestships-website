About EU ETS for Maritime

The EU Emissions Trading System (ETS) was extended to cover maritime transport starting in 2024. Ship operators must surrender emission allowances for their verified CO2 emissions.

The system phases in gradually: 40% of emissions in 2024, 70% in 2025, and 100% from 2026 onwards. This applies to 100% of emissions from voyages within the EU/EEA, and 50% of emissions from voyages into or out of the EU/EEA.

From 2026, methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O) emissions will also be included, converted to CO2-equivalent using global warming potentials.

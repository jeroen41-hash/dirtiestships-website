DirtiestShips.com provides visualisations and analyses of carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions from ships operating in European waters. The information on this website is published for transparency, informational, and analytical purposes only.

Purpose of this website

The purpose of DirtiestShips.com is to make ship-level CO2 emissions data more accessible and understandable, based on officially reported information under European Union regulations.

This website does not assess:

  • Safety or seaworthiness of vessels
  • Crew performance or operational conduct
  • Regulatory compliance beyond emissions reporting
  • Commercial or technical quality of ships or operators

Data source

All emissions data presented on this website is derived from the European Union Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (EU MRV) system for maritime transport, established under Regulation (EU) 2015/757.

  • Data is reported annually by ship operators
  • Applies to ships of 5,000 GT and above
  • Covers voyages to, from, and between EU/EEA ports
  • Data is independently verified by accredited MRV verifiers

Emissions scope and boundaries

The emissions shown represent tank-to-wake CO2 emissions only, as reported under EU MRV.

The data includes:

  • CO2 emissions from onboard fuel combustion
  • Emissions during voyages and port stays within MRV scope

The data excludes:

  • Upstream fuel production emissions (well-to-tank)
  • Non-CO2 greenhouse gases such as methane or nitrous oxide
  • Lifecycle emissions of vessels or onboard equipment
  • Voyages entirely outside the EU MRV reporting scope

Ranking and interpretation

Ships are typically ranked by total annual CO2 emissions. This reflects absolute emissions contribution, not operational efficiency.

Important: High absolute CO2 emissions do not necessarily indicate an inefficient, poorly operated, or non-compliant vessel. Larger ships, vessels with high utilisation, or ships operating long and frequent routes will naturally emit more CO2.

Unless explicitly stated, emissions figures are not normalised for:

  • Cargo carried or payload
  • Passenger numbers
  • Distance sailed
  • Weather conditions
  • Operational speed or routing constraints

Vessel classification

Ships are grouped using standard IMO vessel type classifications where available. Comparisons should only be made within similar vessel types and size categories.

Direct comparisons between different ship types (e.g. ferries vs. tankers) are not analytically meaningful and should be avoided.

Data accuracy and limitations

While EU MRV data is independently verified, it remains based on operator reporting and may be subject to assumptions, revisions, or corrections by the European Commission.

DirtiestShips.com publishes the data as reported and does not modify official emissions figures.

Understanding ship emissions data

Ship Rankings By Ship Type By Company By Country

Does being listed here mean my ship is inefficient or badly operated?

No. Ships on this website are typically ranked by absolute CO2 emissions. High absolute emissions do not imply inefficiency, poor operation, or non-compliance.

Large vessels, ships with high utilisation, and vessels operating long or frequent routes will naturally emit more CO2 than smaller or less active ships.

Why does this site call ships "dirtiest"?

The term "dirtiest" is used to draw attention to total CO2 contribution, not to make moral or technical judgements about individual ships, crews, or operators.

It reflects climate impact in absolute terms, not performance per tonne, mile, or passenger.

Is this data reliable?

The data originates from the EU Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (MRV) system, which requires ship operators to submit annual emissions reports that are independently verified by accredited third parties.

DirtiestShips.com republishes this data as reported and does not alter official figures.

Does this include all greenhouse gas emissions?

No. The data includes tank-to-wake CO2 emissions only, as defined under EU MRV.

It does not include methane, nitrous oxide, or upstream emissions from fuel production (well-to-tank).

Why isn't emissions data normalised per tonne-mile or passenger?

Normalised metrics require additional operational data (cargo carried, distance, passenger numbers) that is not consistently or publicly available at ship level under EU MRV.

Where such metrics are shown, they are clearly labelled. Otherwise, rankings are based on absolute emissions only.

Can I compare different ship types directly?

No. Direct comparisons between different vessel types (e.g. ferries, tankers, bulk carriers, container ships) are not analytically meaningful.

Users should always compare ships within similar vessel types and size categories.

Does this mean my ship is non-compliant with regulations?

No. Inclusion on this website does not indicate regulatory non-compliance. All ships listed have reported emissions as required under EU MRV regulations.

Are charterers or operators identified?

Where available, company names are shown as reported in EU MRV data. This does not imply responsibility, fault, or operational control for emissions outcomes.

What about speed, weather, or routing constraints?

Emissions figures do not adjust for weather conditions, routing limitations, port congestion, or commercial speed requirements.

These factors can significantly influence fuel consumption and emissions.

Can I request corrections or clarifications?

Yes. If you believe vessel data has been misclassified or incorrectly displayed, please contact us with supporting information. We will review all substantiated requests.

Important: This website is intended to support transparency and informed discussion. It should not be used as a standalone performance, compliance, or investment assessment tool.
